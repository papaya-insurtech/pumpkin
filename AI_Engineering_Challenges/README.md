# AI Engineering Challenges

## Why

The way software gets built has fundamentally changed. AI coding tools — Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot — have collapsed the gap between "knowing what to build" and "building it." A product manager can now ship a working prototype. A business analyst can build a data pipeline. An engineer can deliver in hours what used to take days.

This changes what we hire for.

We no longer need to test whether someone can write a for-loop or recall the difference between `Promise.all` and `Promise.allSettled`. We need to test whether someone can **think clearly about a problem, decompose it into actionable steps, and direct AI tools to deliver a working solution.** The skill is no longer typing code — it's knowing what to build, why, and whether the output is correct.

These 15 challenges are designed for that world. Every challenge is:

- **Insurance-domain specific** — not generic TODO apps. Candidates work with real concepts: policies, claims, benefits, fraud detection, regulatory compliance.
- **Self-contained** — no access to our codebase needed. Each challenge includes all the data, schemas, and context required.
- **AI-tool-native** — candidates are expected (not just allowed) to use AI coding tools. We evaluate how effectively they leverage these tools, not whether they memorize syntax.
- **Role-agnostic at the beginner level** — a PM, BA, PO, or engineer can all attempt the beginner challenges. What differs is the depth and quality of their output, not whether they can attempt it at all.

## What We Evaluate

1. **Problem decomposition** — Did they break the problem into clear steps before diving in?
2. **AI tool direction** — Did they give the AI clear, specific instructions? Did they catch and correct mistakes?
3. **Domain understanding** — Do they understand what they built and why it matters in insurance?
4. **Output quality** — Does the solution work? Is it correct? Is it well-presented?
5. **Edge case awareness** — Did they think about what could go wrong?

## How to Assign

- **BA / PO / PM**: Start with challenges 01–05. Advance to 06, 09, or 12 for stronger candidates.
- **Junior Engineer**: Start with 01–05, then 06 or 07.
- **Mid Engineer**: Challenges 06–10.
- **Senior Engineer**: Challenges 11–15.
- **AI/ML Engineer**: Challenges 08, 10, 11.

Pick **one** challenge per candidate. The challenge should be slightly above what you expect them to handle comfortably — we want to see how they navigate difficulty, not confirm what they already know.
